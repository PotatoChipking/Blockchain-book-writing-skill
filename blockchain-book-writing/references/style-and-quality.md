# Style and Quality

## Writing Style

Use formal technical book style:

- Clear, steady, and accurate language.
- Logical progression rather than term stacking.
- Prefer "problem background -> technical mechanism -> engineering implementation -> application value -> limits and trends".
- Avoid exaggeration, marketing, slogans, and unsupported certainty.
- Do not present business vision as technical fact.
- Do not present a single project as an unconditional success case.
- Keep uncertainty analysis for emerging projects.

## Recommended Expressions

Prefer expressions such as:

- "从技术演进角度看……"
- "这一设计反映出……"
- "其核心并不在于……而在于……"
- "在企业级场景中，关键问题通常不是……而是……"
- "与传统公链相比，该模式更强调……"
- "与传统联盟链相比，该方案试图补足……"
- "这类设计的价值，需要放在……背景下理解。"
- "该方向仍处于发展阶段，其长期影响取决于……"
- "对于金融基础设施而言，性能并不是唯一指标，合规、隐私、审计和互操作性同样重要。"

Avoid expressions such as:

- "彻底颠覆"
- "革命性改变"
- "完美解决"
- "必然取代"
- "没有任何风险"
- "唯一答案"
- "最强技术"
- "划时代突破"
- "全新范式已经形成"
- "毫无疑问会成为未来主流"

## Length Rules

If the user specifies a target length, stay close to it. If not:

- 小节说明：约 800-1500 字
- 普通章节：约 2000-4000 字
- 重点章节：约 5000-10000 字
- 目录摘要：约 200-500 字
- 章节大纲：根据复杂度输出二级或三级标题

For expansion, add background, mechanisms, engineering details, comparisons, use cases, and limits. For compression, preserve the core argument, key mechanisms, conclusions, and relationship to the book.

## Common Problems to Avoid

- Writing product promotion, social-media marketing, encyclopedia entries, or loose bullet lists.
- Explaining only "what" without "why" and "how".
- Listing terms without relationships.
- Turning business vision into technical reality.
- Understating regulatory or compliance problems.
- Mixing stablecoins, tokenized deposits, RWA, CBDC, and digital assets.
- Making unsupported definite claims about new projects.
- Exaggerating performance, security, or adoption.
- Starting every chapter with the same sentence pattern.
- Overusing "首先、其次、最后".
- Entering technical detail abruptly without context.
- Using too many unexplained English abbreviations.
- Making prose obscure just to appear professional.

## Final Quality Checklist

Before final output, check:

- Title: Does the text answer the given title and core concept?
- Structure: Does it have a clear opening, progression, and ending?
- Accuracy: Are concepts accurate and not mixed? Are uncertain claims marked?
- Style: Does it read like a formal book chapter rather than a blog or promotion?
- Engineering depth: Does it include architecture, mechanisms, flows, or implementation where relevant?
- Book consistency: Does it fit the parent topic and adjacent chapters? Are terms consistent?
- Risk: Are unverified data, hype, factual-risk areas, and missing source needs handled?
